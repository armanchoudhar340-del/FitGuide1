# Hammer Curl Biceps Workout

## Hammer Curl Exercise Variations

### 1. Classic Hammer Curl
- **Muscles**: Biceps, Forearms
- **Sets**: 3
- **Reps**: 12
- **Equipment**: Dumbbells
- **Instructions**: Hold dumbbells with palms facing inward (neutral grip). Curl up keeping wrists neutral.

### 2. Alternating Hammer Curl
- **Muscles**: Biceps, Forearms
- **Sets**: 3
- **Reps**: 10 per arm
- **Equipment**: Dumbbells
- **Instructions**: Curl one arm at a time while the other arm rests.

### 3. Hammer Curl with Resistance Band
- **Muscles**: Biceps, Forearms
- **Sets**: 3
- **Reps**: 15
- **Equipment**: Resistance Band
- **Instructions**: Stand on band with neutral grip, curl keeping palms facing each other.

### 4. Cable Hammer Curl
- **Muscles**: Biceps, Forearms
- **Sets**: 3
- **Reps**: 12
- **Equipment**: Cable Machine
- **Instructions**: Use cable with rope attachment, maintain neutral grip throughout.

### 5. Incline Hammer Curl
- **Muscles**: Biceps (long head focus)
- **Sets**: 3
- **Reps**: 12
- **Equipment**: Dumbbells, Incline Bench
- **Instructions**: Perform hammer curls on incline bench to stretch biceps.

## Complete Biceps Workout Routine

### Warm-up (5 minutes)
- Arm circles: 30 seconds each direction
- Biceps stretch: 30 seconds
- Wrist rotations: 30 seconds

### Main Workout
1. **Barbell Bicep Curl** - 3 sets × 10-12 reps
2. **Hammer Curl** - 3 sets × 12 reps
3. **Alternating Hammer Curl** - 3 sets × 10 per arm
4. **Concentration Curl** - 3 sets × 12 per arm
5. **Preacher Curl (or Resistance Band Curl)** - 3 sets × 10-12 reps

### Cool Down (5 minutes)
- Biceps stretch: 60 seconds
- Forearm stretch: 30 seconds each arm

## Benefits of Hammer Curls
- **Builds Biceps Mass**: Targets both short and long head of biceps
- **Forearm Development**: Strengthens forearm muscles
- **Grip Strength**: Improves overall grip endurance
- **Joint Health**: Neutral grip reduces wrist stress
- **Functional Movement**: Transfers to real-world pulling motions

## Workout Tips
1. Keep elbows stationary, only forearms move
2. Control the negative (eccentric) phase
3. Use full range of motion
4. Focus on squeeze at the top
5. Breathe out on the curl, breathe in on the return
